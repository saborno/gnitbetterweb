Course We Offer

We are offer Five of the best Technical course for our student by GNIT

BTECH(Electronics and Communication engineering)

B.Tech in Electronics and Communication Engineering (ECE) is a four-year undergraduate course that focuses on the design, development, and application of electronic circuits and communication systems. It equips students with knowledge and skills in electronics, communication, and software engineering.

BTECH(Computer Science Engineering)

B.Tech in Computer Science Engineering (CSE) is a four-year undergraduate course that focuses on the design, development, and application of computer systems and software. It covers programming languages, data structures, algorithms, computer architecture, and software engineering, preparing students for careers in software development, artificial intelligence, and data science.

BTECH(Food Technology)

B.Tech in Food Technology is a four-year undergraduate course that combines food science, technology, and engineering to develop safe, nutritious, and appealing food products, with a focus on processing, preservation, and packaging.

BTECH(Information Technology)

B.Tech in Information Technology (IT) is a four-year undergraduate course that focuses on the design, development, and management of information systems, emphasizing networking, database management, and software applications.

BTECH(Electronics and Computer Science)

B.Tech in Electronics and Computer Science (ECS) is a four-year undergraduate course that combines electronics and computer science to develop skills in designing, developing, and integrating electronic systems, software, and hardware.

Our Campus

The GNIT campus, spread over a sprawling 28,328 square meters, is a vibrant and eco-friendly oasis that embodies the institution's commitment to sustainability and environmental stewardship. This green campus is a tranquil retreat from the hustle and bustle of city life, providing a unique and inspiring learning environment for its students. The campus is meticulously designed to minimize its carbon footprint, with an abundance of lush greenery, trees, and plants that help to purify the air and create a natural ambiance. The buildings are constructed using eco-friendly materials and are designed to maximize natural light and ventilation, reducing the need for artificial lighting and heating/cooling systems. The campus features a range of sustainable initiatives, including rainwater harvesting systems, solar panels, and waste management facilities, which help to conserve resources and reduce waste. The institution has also implemented a number of energy-efficient measures, such as LED lighting and smart energy management systems, to minimize its energy consumption.
